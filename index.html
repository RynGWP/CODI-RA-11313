Overview

A Law That Defends Your Dignity

Republic Act 11313, also known as the Safe Spaces Act or "Bawal Bastos Law," was signed into law on April 17, 2019. It expands the scope of anti-sexual harassment laws to cover gender-based harassment in all public and online spaces — not just in workplaces or schools.

The law recognizes that everyone deserves to move freely, speak boldly, and exist safely regardless of gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, or expression.

Prohibited Acts
Key Provisions

What the Law Prohibits

The Safe Spaces Act explicitly covers a wide range of conduct across multiple environments.

🚶

Public Spaces

Cat-calling, wolf-whistling, unwanted invitations, misogynistic slurs, cursing, and persistent telling of sexual jokes in streets, alleys, public parks, and similar spaces.

💻

Online Spaces

Unwanted sexual remarks, videos, photos, or messages sent through electronic means; cyberharassment; and any gender-based online sexual harassment.

🏢

Workplaces

Employers must adopt policies and mechanisms against gender-based harassment. Sexual favors demanded in exchange for employment, promotion, or benefits are strictly prohibited.

🏫

Educational Institutions

Schools and universities must adopt internal mechanisms and provide a safe reporting process for students, faculty, and staff experiencing harassment.

⚖️

Penalties

Fines range from ₱1,000 to ₱500,000 and imprisonment of 6 days to 6 years depending on the gravity, frequency, and location of the offense.

🛡️

Bystander Protection

The law encourages bystander intervention and protects those who assist victims from any form of retaliation or counter-suit.

Coverage

Everyone Is Protected

The Safe Spaces Act protects all persons regardless of the following characteristics:

Gender Identity Sexual Orientation Gender Expression Sex Characteristics Women Men LGBTQIA+ Persons Students Employees Faculty & Staff Persons with Disabilities Senior Citizens
How To Report

Filing a Complaint

Your safety matters. Here is how to formally report a Safe Spaces Act violation through CODI.

Reach Out

Contact CODI via hotline 0968 181 0942 or email codi@usm.edu.ph. You may also visit the CODI office personally.

File a Report

Complete the official complaint form. Document the incident with as much detail as possible — date, time, location, and witnesses.

Investigation

CODI will assess the complaint and coordinate with the appropriate committee for a fact-finding investigation within the prescribed period.

Resolution

A hearing will be conducted. Sanctions, disciplinary action, or referral to law enforcement will follow based on findings.
